Programming Robots

Resources
  • Microbit Makecode - Integrated Development Environment (IDE)
  • Microbit Maqueen educational robot documentation

Part I

Explore
  • How to move forward, backward, and turn
  • How to display a message or symbol on the LED matrix
  • How to play a sound
  • How to repeat doing the above actions a specific number of times
Challenge #1

Create an algorithm that tells your robot how to celebrate, incorporating at least the following:

  • Wheel movement
  • LED Matrix display
  • Audio sounds
  • Repeat some (but not all) of these actions a specific number of times (not indefinitely)
  • Entire celebration should take between 3-10 seconds

Part II

Explore
  • What information does the light sensor provide?
  • What does it indicate on light surfaces? Dark surfaces?
Challenge #2

Program your robot to do the following:

  • When it turns on, begin driving forward
  • Continue driving forward until it reaches a surface of the opposite color (light/dark)
  • Once it has reached the opposite surface, stop moving forward
  • Begin your robot's celebration (from Challenge 1)!

Part III

Explore
  • What information does the ultrasonic sensor provide?
  • If the robot is driving toward an obstacle, what is a good distance to detect to stop the robot before colliding?
Challenge #3

Program your robot to do the following:

  • When it turns on, begin driving forward
  • Continue driving forward until it comes close to the obstacle ahead of it, but stops before colliding.

Part IV

Explore
  • What actions will make the robot right-angle turn (90 degrees), from a stand-still?
  • After turning, how can the robot make sure it doesn't run into any obstacles in the new direction?
Challenge #4

Program your robot to do the following:

  • Begin the robot on one side of your bridge, facing the opposite side. However, place an obstacle in the way.
  • The robot should drive toward the obstacle, but once it gets close to it, stop moving forward.
  • Next, the robot should turn 90-degrees (either left or right) and move in that direction.
  • Before running into the side of the bridge, the robot should stop and then turn to face its original direction.
  • After facing the original direction, if there is clearance to the end of the bridge, drive across the rest of the bridge

Part V

Explore
Final Challenge #5

Combined your challenges to do the following:

  • Begin the robot on one side of your bridge, facing the opposite side. It should reach a contrasting surface at the end and stop.
  • Once the robot reaches the contrasting surface, it should perform its celebration (from Challenge #1).
  • However, along the way across the bridge, if there is an obstacle, the robot should move around it without touching it.
  • For an extra challenge, try to program it to work no matter where the obstacle is placed. For example, the obstacle might be near the front, back, left, or right side of the bridge... or there may be no obstacle at all. The extra challenge should work in all circumstances.
